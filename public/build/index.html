Support to Ganga Rejuvenation

The River Ganga is a source of livelihood for over 500 million people. It provides drinking water and is the basis for a range of economic and agricultural activities. An intact river landscape also offers a habitat for diverse flora and fauna. The Ganges is also of considerable cultural and religious significance.

Efforts to clean the Ganga go back almost 30 years and have included different strategic approaches, cooperation arrangements, projects and investments, donor support, as well as studies and analyses. Despite the various strategies, pollution in the Ganges has not decreased, in some areas it has, indeed, increased.


In 2014 the Indian Government approved the “Namami Gange Programme”, as an integrated conservation mission, to accomplish the objective of effective abatement of pollution, conservation and rejuvenation of the Ganga.


The Indo-German bilateral cooperation project “Support to Ganga Rejuvenation” in conjunction with the “Implementation of the India EU Water Partnership” cooperates with the Namami Gange programme with the overall objective to support responsible stakeholders at national and state level to increasingly apply integrated approaches to river basin management taking EU methods and experience into account, to facilitate cooperation between India and EU Member States on water-related issues and to help create business opportunities for EU companies having the technical know-how to contribute to improving the efficiency, effectiveness and sustainability of water management in India.


The thrust of the actions is on river basins with a focus on the Ganga basin. Measures at regional level target the states of Uttarakhand and Uttar Pradesh. The project partners are the Central Government, state governments and selected municipalities. At national level, the measure adopts a multi-sectoral approach which considers not only the problem of sewage generated by human settlements and industrial wastewater but also other pressure factors relevant for river basin management.
